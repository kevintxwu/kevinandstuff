The Resistance
Mobile Adaptation

The Resistance is a popular social deduction board game. The physical copy has sold over 3.5 million dollars in revenue. I recruited a group of developers and partnered with the publisher to build an official mobile adaptation.

View Site View Documents

It all started with a cold email.

“Hi, I’m a student at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. I play Resistance a lot with friends and family, and it's one of my favorite board games due to it's energy and depth. Currently, I play resistance in a group coincidentally with a lot of talented developers and designers, and so I thought, why not build an app for it, so that we could play the game whenever we wanted? We definitely have the skill set to do it. So I was wondering if Indie Boards And Cards and Don Eskridge are interested?”

From there, the publisher, Travis Worthington of Indie Boards and Cards agreed, and an adventure began.

The Resistance was my company Unboard’s first project, and our first experience with a big client. We started development on October 2014, and recently released the beta version of The Resistance to an exclusive audience.

We started off on the wrong foot. I tried to create a sleek, modern design but eventually realized I had lost the feel of The Resistance in translation. Meanwhile, we hit obstacle after obstacle in development. The final goal was to have two native apps interfacing with the same web server. We quickly realized that keeping two teams (Android and iOS) in sync while building a server in between required more than just basic teamwork. So, I put some more thought into it.

With everything I learned in mind, I was able to design a feel and flow that was modern and fitting. At the same time, we organized and buckled down, using a mix of emails, Trello, and a 30 page design document to literally keep everyone on the same page.

After a year of late nights, weekly meetings, and hundreds of hours of coding and debugging, we’re finally ready for an official release by Thanksgiving 2015!
