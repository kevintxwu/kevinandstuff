Epic Everything
Collectible Card Game

I led a team of over 30 people in building and marketing a real-time collectible card game packed with adventure and strategy. We raised over $40,000 dollars before pivoting and ultimately deciding to open source the project.

View Trailer View Kickstarter View Site View Repo

For more about Epic Everything itself, please go to the Kickstarter campaign! It does a pretty good job of explaining everything about it.

The spark for Epic Everything started with a hackathon project. It was an incomplete iOS game that turned into something more. We continued working on it with Unity instead to create a more fully featured game.

I managed a global team, from local Berkeley developers, to marketing associates across North America, to concept artists from Russia. We ended up with not only a beautiful game, but an extensive world and lore.

Approaching the end of our crowdfunding campaign, it was clear we would make the funding goal, but only barely. As a result, we were faced with a difficult decision. Would we be able to make the game with barely enough funding to go by, and risk a sub-par, meaningless product? In light of that realization, we decided that though we were unable to procure the funds to complete the game, we did receive a lot support from the community, so we open sourced what we had and put our own personal development of the game on pause for now.
