Starting from February 2014, I worked at Salesforce developing a selection of open source mobile apps to demonstrate their newest platform: Salesforce1.

View RollCall View Tasky View CheckList View ListMe

I worked in a team with my good friend Howard Chen, under my favorite boss ever Sandeep Bhanot, building apps using Visualforce, Apex, and AngularJS.

The photo above is of our very first project, RollCall. RollCall is a check-in system for company events, based on Salesforce's existing employee and customer databases. Overall, we built four different apps over our tenure at Salesforce that expanded on existing functions already in Salesforce.

The two apps shown above are Tasky and CheckList, our final two apps. By this time, Howard and I were at maximum efficiency, churning out apps in nearly single month length cycles.

One thing you'll notice is that all the apps have the same blue navigation bar on the top of them. Since these apps have to be used within Salesforce1, I had to design them around the Salesforce1 navigation system. This meant bright colors to match the bright blue bar and equally minimalist, flat UI.

The four apps we created: RollCall, Inspector, Tasky, and ListMe were scheduled to be released on Salesforce's app store AppExchange.
