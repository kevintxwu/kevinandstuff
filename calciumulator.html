Calciumulator
Hackathon Project

At the Design For Good Hackathon, my team and I won first place by building the Calciumulator for the American Bone Health Organization. They needed a calculator designed to make it easy and painless for people to quickly estimate the amount of calcium in their diet.

View Site View Repo

The biggest problem we had to solve with the Calciumulator was not technical. With some rudimentary javascript and knowledge of basic nutrition, itâ€™s easy to implement a calculator for daily calcium intake. The biggest problem we had to solve was how to make it fun and easy. How do we add personality to something as drab and boring as calcium nutrition?

In the first few hours of our hackathon we realized the answer was simple: we can add personality with an actual personality. Thus, Skelly was born.

Skelly is a silly, cheesy, and maybe slightly annoying living skeleton. He wants to make you laugh while making sure you're healthy. Using subtle drop shadows and bright colors, we designed the Calciumulator to feel like pieces of construction paper pasted on a blue backdrop. The whole experience feels like an arts and crafts project. Whatever we did we did to counteract the monotony of a daily nutrition check.

He pops his head out at various stages of the Calciumulator, constantly keeping the user company. We kind of imagined him playing the same role as the GitHub Octocat, except even more out there.

Thankfully, by the time we all presented our projects at the end of the hackathon, the judges must've seen something in Skelly's silly smile. We proudly shipped off our Calciumulator to the American Bone Health organization with the hope that it'd make someone laugh somewhere while perhaps giving them some healthy advice.
